Algebra

Algebra is a part of mathematics dealing with symbols and the rules
for manipulating those symbols. In elementary algebra, those symbols
(wich are written as letters) represent quantities withought fixed
values, these are known as variables.

Example:

1 + a = 2
What is a?



But algebra isnt alwase that easy, you can get more complex equations
like

5a - 20 = (10 / 2)

To solve this you need to eliminate everything other than the algebraic
symbols in the equation, so using that last question as an example you need
to simplify the equation to just leave the 5a. This is how:

5a - 20 = (10 / 2) ↓ Simplify

5a - 20 = 5 ↓ Add 20 to both sides

5a = 25 ↓ Devide by 5 on each side

a = 5

And thats algebra sometimes there is more that one symbol like a and b
but they work like this

2a + 10 = b + 10 ↓ Take away 10 from both sides

2a = b ↓ Devide both side by 2

a = b/2

Sometimes in algebra they dont give perfect answeres, as displayed on top.

Remember to simplify and alwase whatever
you do to one side do to the other.

    Powers

    In this whole revision Powers are the most simple to do, lets show some examples:

    2 ^ 2 = 4 ( 2 X 2 = 4 )
    2 ^ 3 = 8 ( 2 X 2 X 2 = 8 )
    3 ^ 2 = 9 ( 3 x 3 = 9 )

    Basically the Power (the number in the top right of the base number) is how many times
    you times the main number by itself so ^2 = (num X num) and ^3 = (num X num X num), its
    quite simple.

    Test:
    5 ^ 3 = ?


    Even though powers may seem simple, they get more dificult when you start going into
    negatives. Like 5 ^ -2 = ?, or -5 ^ 2.

    A little tip:
    5 ^ 2 = 25
    -5 ^ 2 = 25

    A negative number squared, cubed ect... Is the same as its posative counterpart

    The problems start when you have things like 5 ^ -2 = ?.
    Say we have the problem:
    4 ^ -2 = ?

    We can use whats called the reciprical of that to get:
    4 ^ -2 = 1 / 4 ^ 2

    Then we can work out 4 ^ 2 wich is 16 so we can simplify the equation:
    4 ^ -2 = 1 / 16

    Then we work out what 1 / 16 is, 0.0625, then we can plug that into our equation:
    4 ^ -2 = 0.0625

    Propability

    In mathematics Propobility is the likelyhood or chance of an event occurring.

    Propobility = the number of ways of achieving success / the total number of possible outcomes

    For example, the probability of flipping a coin and it being heads is ½, because there is 1 way
    of getting a head and the total number of possible outcomes is 2 (a head or tail). We write P(heads)
    = ½.

    The probability of something which is certain to happen is 1.
    The probability of something which is impossible to happen is 0.
    The probability of something not happening is 1 minus the probability that it will happen.

    Example:

    There is a bag full of coloured balls, red, blue, green and orange. Balls are picked out and replaced.
    John did this 1000 times and obtained the following results:

    Number of blue balls picked out: 300
    Number of red balls: 200
    Number of green balls: 450
    Number of orange balls: 50

    a) What is the propobility of picking a green ball?

    Well as said for every 1000 balls picked out, 450 are green. Therefore P(green) = 450/1000 = 0.45
    45%

    b) If there are 100 balls in the bag, how many of them are likely to be green?
    To do this you need to know at least basic ratio click here to look at it more.
    So you know out of the 1000 balls picked 450 of them where green so if there are only 100 picked (1000 / 10
    = 100, 450 / 10 = 45), you will get 45 green balls.

    Pythagoras

    Pythagoras is a formula for working out the third side of a triangle using the other 2 sides.
    First you need to know the names of the sides of the triangle:

    Hypotenuse · The longest side of the triangle.

    Opposite and Adjacent · The other 2 sides of the triangle.

    The Pythagoras formula is a^2 + b^2 = c^2, clickhere to learn more on powers.

    In the formula the a, b and c are placeholders for the length of each side, clickhere to learn more on algebra.

    So if you had a triangle with the sides 3cm, 4cm and 5cm:


    For this we can fill in a with 3, b with 4 and c is missing, (the order doesnt matter) and then we can
    use the formula to work out the missing length (in this case the Hypotenuse).

    a^2 + b^2 = c^2
    3^2 + 4^2 = c^2
    9 + 16 = c^2
    25 = c^2

    So now we know that the length of the Hypotenuse ^ 2 = 25, so we can square root it to find the
    actual length of the Hypotenuse:

    √c^2 = c
    √25 = 5
    5 = c

    Now we add back our measurements, 5 = 5cm, then we worked out the missing length of this
    triangle

    Now this doesnt only work trying to find the Hypotenuse you can use it to find one
    of the smaller sides like the Adjacent or Opposite.


    So in this case we are missing b.
    Lengths:

    a = 3
    b = ?
    c = 5

    So if we plug these into the formula:

    3^2 + ?^2 = 5^2
    9 + ?^2 = 25 (we can take 9 away from both sides)
    b^2 = 16
    √16 = 4
    b = 4

    Ratio

    A ratio is the comparison of 2 numbers, for example if the ratio of 2 lengths
    is 1:2, then that means the second length is twice as big as the first.

    Example:

    Simon made a scale model of a car on a scale of 1:10. The height of the model car is 10cm.

    a) work out the height of the real car.
    To do this we need to know the ratio of toy:car wich is 1:10 so the car is 10 X the height
    of the toy, we know the toys height is 10cm so 10 X 10 = 100cm, the height of the car is 100cm

    b) The length of the real car is 500cm. Work out the length of the model car.
    We know the ratio of toy:car is 1:10 so if the car's length = 500cm, then that makes the toy's
    length 500/10 = 50cm

    so the dimentions of the toy = 50cm by 10cm : car = 500cm by 100cm

    Sequences / N'th Term

    In mathematics a sequence is basically a pattern of numbers.

    Example:

    2, 4, 6, 8, 10
    This is a sequence.

    The question may ask to continue the sequence, if so you need to find the pattern
    of the sequence (in the example it is +2, because it goes up 2 every time), to do so
    you need to find the difference of 2 adjacent numbers like in the example we can use
    2 and 4, the difference being 2, then we can do the same with 4 and 6, difference = 2
    when you know the difference apply it to the last number of the sequence to get the next
    number like in the example, we know it goes up by 2 so it would continue, 12, 14, 16, 18...

    Closely linking with sequences is N'th Term wich is slightly more confusing.
    N = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5...
    2N = 2, 4, 6, 8, 10...
    3N = 3, 6, 9, 12, 15...

    Basically N = the posative number line and saying something like 2N just means for every
    number on the posative number line times it by 2 as shown on those examples

    The N'th Term doesnt only have to be timesed it can be added to:
    N+1 = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6...
    N+2 = 3, 4, 5, 6, 7...
    2N-1 = 1, 3, 5, 7, 9...

    A question may give you a sequence and ask you for the N'th term of it.
    Example of this:

    Find the N'th Term of this sequence.
    5, 10, 15, 20, 25...

    If we compare it to N we can see whats happening.
    1, 2, 3, 4, 5
    5, 10, 15, 20, 25

    So as you can see they are being timesed by 5.
    So the N'th Term would be 5N.

    Test question:

    What is the N'th Term of this Sequence:
    4, 9, 14, 19, 24...
